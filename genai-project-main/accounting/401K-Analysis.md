### **Key Mechanics & Requirements**

#### **1. Employee Roth Deferral ($30,500)**
- **Source**: Your W-2 wages from your S-Corp
- **Timing**: Must be contributed throughout 2025 (or by 12/31/2025)
- **How it works**: 
  - You elect to defer $30,500 of your salary into the Roth 401(k)
  - This amount still shows on your W-2 as taxable income
  - You pay income tax on it in 2025
  - It grows tax-free forever
  - Withdrawals after 59½ are 100% tax-free (contributions + earnings)

#### **2. Employer Profit-Sharing ($14,250)**
- **Source**: S-Corp contribution (separate from your salary)
- **Timing**: Can be made until your S-Corp tax filing deadline (3/17/2026 or 9/15/2026 with extension)
- **Calculation**: Generally 20% of your W-2 wages (approximately)
- **How it works**:
  - S-Corp makes a direct contribution to your 401(k)
  - Must be Traditional (pre-tax) - **Roth is NOT allowed for employer contributions**
  - S-Corp deducts this on Form 1120-S, Line 17 (Pension, profit-sharing plans)
  - Grows tax-deferred; taxed as ordinary income when withdrawn
