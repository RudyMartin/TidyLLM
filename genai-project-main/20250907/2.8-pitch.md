Exactly 👌 — making **Custom Checks an input every time they run the process** is the cleanest way to:

* Give them the **illusion of full control** (they get to “engineer” their commentary).
* Avoid you having to maintain/edit their playground questions.
* Keep the **core Checklist stable** and locked.

---

# 🔑 How It Works

1. **Inputs per run:**

   * Their review docs (PDF, Word, etc.).
   * The **official Checklist** (you provide, frozen).
   * Their **Custom Checks file** (Excel/CSV/JSON), optional.

2. **Processing:**

   * System always runs the **official Checklist**.
   * If a **Custom Checks file** is uploaded:

     * Parse those questions.
     * Run commentary extraction.
     * Add results to the *Custom Checks* section of the report.

3. **Outputs:**

   * **Report** =

     * Checklist (Y/N + evidence)
     * Custom Checks (commentary from their file, clearly marked)
     * Findings & counts

# 📄 Report Example

**Official Checklist**
✔ Data validated (p. 12)
❌ Governance owner missing

**Custom Checks (Uploaded by Client)**

– Were alternative models considered? → *Not documented*

# 🛡 Benefits

* **You:** No ongoing nightmare of managing their experiments.
* **Them:** Full control over what extra commentary shows up.
* **Governance:** Official Checklist never changes; Custom Checks always isolated.
* **Flexibility:** They can upload a new file each time if they want to “play,” or skip it entirely.
