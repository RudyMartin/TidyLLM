1. **"Draft Report of the Joint California Policy Working Group on AI Frontier Models":**
- In September 2024, Governor Gavin Newsom requested that Fei-Fei Li, Co-Director of Stanford HAI; Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar, President of the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace; and Jennifer Tour Chayes, Dean of the UC Berkeley College of Computing, Data Science, and Society, prepare a report focused on AI frontier models. This report released by the Co-Leads and collaborators of the Joint California Policy Working Group on AI Frontier Models leverages broad evidence to provide a framework for policymaking on frontier AI, 
      taking into account the importance of innovation and establishing appropriate strategies to reduce material risks. [Link](https://www.cafrontieraigov.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Draft_Report_of_the_Joint_California_Policy_Working_Group_on_AI_Frontier_Models.pdf)

3. **"Eliciting Domain Expertise in the Absence of Formal Authority:"**
-  The Case of AI Developers and Domain Experts in a Large Firm
This Stanford  University  study explores how AI developers without formal authority can successfully elicit domain expertise from organizational experts, based on a four-year field study at a multinational fashion firm. It finds that elicitation is more effective when tasks are central, clearly defined, and consistently enacted, highlighting how task and organizational structures shape access to and clarity of domain expertise. 
 [Link](https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/AI_Developers_Domain_Experts_Formal_Authority.pdf)
