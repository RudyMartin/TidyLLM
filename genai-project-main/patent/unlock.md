What this invention unlocks

This invention enables interoperable decision logic across intelligent systems by providing a common, machine-consumable causal signal of context integrity. Rather than encoding responses or policies, the system standardizes how contextual reliability is measured and communicated, allowing heterogeneous systems to make compatible decisions based on shared semantics. This creates a stable interface that remains valid as models, architectures, and execution environments evolve. The signal can be consumed by control planes, agents, networks, governance layers, or human operators without coupling to a specific domain or response strategy. As a result, the invention supports coordinated behavior across distributed, probabilistic, quantum-adjacent, and future computational systems, including applications and product categories that have not yet emerged.
